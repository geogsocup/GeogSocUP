Principles

UP GeogSoc is guided by 4 main principles: Academic Excellence, Camaraderie, Service, and Advocacy.

Academic Excellence

UP GeogSoc has been at the forefront of academic excellence, producing academic achievers and organizing scholarly events.

Camaraderie

Throughout the years, lasting friendships were formed in the organization. Even though it has been in different places over the past few years, the org tambayan has also served as a haven and safe space for UP GeogSoc's members, where they have spent their time studying, playing, planning, meeting, and even sleeping.

Service

The organization has remained committed to be of service to the UP community and to the nation as well, using geographic tools and concepts in various community building projects and various initiatives.

Advocacy

The organization advocates for the holistic discipline of Geography at the forefront of local, national, and global development. Geography and its subfields such as cartography, GIS, medical geogrpahy, meteorology, and urban planning are powerful tools that could be used to help build communities and make a better society.

Events

To help promote the discipline of Geography, UP GeogSoc organizes various events that showcase and highlight the social relevance and importance of Geography.

Geog Ride

A semestral field trip.

Geog Ride is a semestral field trip, specifically catering to Geog 1 students but also open to all college students in UP Diliman, that has been providing students an opportunity to gauge their understanding of their lessons in Geography, test the skills they have learned during class discussions, and apply them in real world scenarios. It also aims to make them perceptive specifically on the need for proper resource management and the advantages of sustainable tourism. Geog Ride also focuses on the cultural geography of places and on the appreciation of different landmarks and landscapes. Geog Ride destinations in the past include Pangasinan, La Union, Zambales, Bataan, Nueva Ecija, Batangas, Quezon and even Manila.

Philippine Geography Olympiad

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

The Philippine Geography Olympiad (PGO) is the premier and largest inter-high school Geography quiz bee in the country. PGO is recognized by the International Geography Olympiad (iGEO) of the International Geographical Union (IGU). It is a nationwide competition, with over 100 schools from the different parts of the country participating in recent years. The winner and the 1st runner-up become the Philippines’ representatives at the iGEO. Since UP GeogSoc’s affiliation with IAYG in 2018, the IAYG has helped in organizing the event and has provided additional resources and support to UP GeogSoc. The IAYG has also helped and given logistical support to the Filipino representatives at the iGEO. The latest edition of PGO, the 10th Philippine Geography Olympiad (also known as PGO X), led by UP GeogSoc member and event Chairperson Niel Adrian Babay, was held for two days from 07 March to 08 March 2020. This was the first time that the event was held for two days and the first time that the event featured a field exam, where participants were given the chance to experience on-the-field application of geographic concepts.
